What we do

Projectile motion is a form of motion experienced by an object or particle that is thrown near the Earth's surface and moves along a curved path under the action of gravity.

Hypothesis

We hypothesize that, in the absence of air resistance, objects of different masses will fall at the same rate and hit the ground at the same time. This challenges the common misconception that heavier objects fall faster than lighter ones. According to physics principles, all objects experience the same gravitational acceleration regardless of mass.
